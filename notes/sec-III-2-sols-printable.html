    4. Definitions: Power, Polynomial, and Rational Functions.

    ðŸ”—
    Solution.
    1. A power function is a function which has the form \(f(x) = x^p\) for some \(p \in \R\text{.}\) \(p\) is called the power. Note that we did not specify the domain as this may change depending on \(p\text{.}\)
      ðŸ”—
      ðŸ”—
    2. A polynomial function is a sum of constant multiples of power functions with non-negative integer powers. That is, they are functions \(p: \R \to \R\) where
      \begin{equation*} p(x)= c_0 + c_1x + c_2x^2+ \dots + c_nx^n = \sum_{i=0}^n c_i x^i\text{.} \end{equation*}
      The power \(n\) is called the degree of \(p(x)\text{.}\)
      ðŸ”—
      ðŸ”—
