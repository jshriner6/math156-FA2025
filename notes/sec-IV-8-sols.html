    Worksheet \(\S IV.8\text{:}\) Arithmetic Sums
    

    1. Motivation: Arithmetic Sums.

    🔗
    Solution.
    Arithmetic series aren’t useful, but partial sums are. How can we utilize their structure to compute them efficiently?
    🔗
    🔗
    🔗

    2. Definition: Arithmetic Sequence.

    An arithmetic sequence is a sequence of the form
    🔗
    Solution.
    \begin{equation*} a, a + d, a+2d, a+3d, \dots\text{.} \end{equation*}
    The rule for a generic term is \(a_n = a + nd\text{.}\)
    🔗
    🔗
    🔗

    3. Example 1: Sums of Arithmetic Sequences.

    Consider the sequence
    \begin{equation*} 0,1,2,3,4,\dots, k \end{equation*}
    which has the corresponding sum \(\sum_{n=0}^k n \text{.}\)
    🔗
    Solution.
    We can use the structure of this sequence to compute that
    \begin{equation*} \sum_{n=0}^k n = \frac{k(k+1)}{2}\text{.} \end{equation*}
    See our class notes or your textbook for details.
    🔗
    🔗
    🔗

    4. Example 2: Sums of Arithmetic Sequences.

    Consider the sum \(\sum_{n=0}^{100} (2 + 3n) \text{.}\)
    🔗
    Solution.
    We can use the structure of this sequence to compute that
    \begin{equation*} \sum_{n=0}^{100} (2 + 3n) =2 \dot (101) + \frac{3(100)(101)}{2}\text{.} \end{equation*}
    See our class notes or your textbook for details.
    🔗
    🔗
    🔗

    5. Practice: Sums of Arithmetic Sequences.

    Compute the following sums:
    1. \(\displaystyle \sum_{n=0}^{50} (4 + 7n) \)
      🔗
      🔗
    2. \(\displaystyle \sum_{n=1}^{70} (-6 + 2n) \)
      🔗
      🔗
    3. \(\displaystyle \sum_{n=10}^{60} (2 - 3n) \)
      🔗
      🔗
    As a challenge, compute a formula for the generic sum
    \begin{equation*} \sum_{n=0}^k ( a+ dn)\text{.} \end{equation*}
    🔗
    Solution.
    1. \(\displaystyle \sum_{n=0}^{50} (4 + 7n) = 4\cdot (51) + \frac{7(50)(51)}{2} \)
      🔗
      🔗
    2. \(\displaystyle \sum_{n=1}^{70} (-6 + 2n) = -6 \cdot (70) + \frac{2(70)(71)}{2} \)
      🔗
      🔗
    3. \(\displaystyle \sum_{n=10}^{60} (2 - 3n) = 2 \cdot (51) -3 \cdot \frac{70(51)}{2} \)
      🔗
      🔗
    See our class notes for details.
